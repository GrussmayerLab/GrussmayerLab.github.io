GRUSSMAYER LAB

Microscopy to understand the dynamics of life at the nanoscale

Life, on fundamental level, consists of interactions between individual molecules, forming ever larger and more complex structures. The interaction of these molecules and subsequent structures lead to intricate and arbitrarily complex mechanisms. We are a team of engineers, physicists and biologists, with the goal to develop advanced 3D light microscopy methods to perform quantitative studies in live cells to answer fundamental questions in molecular and cell biology. We combine label-free and molecule-specific super-resolution fluorescence readouts to assess mechanical properties, dynamics and structure. Join us in our path to make these developments, as well as the work of the vast imaging community, available to the world.

The lab

The Grussmayer Lab

Our interdisciplinary Bionanoscience group builds upon synergies from molecular/cell biology, (physical/bio) chemistry, (bio)physics and optics. We develop cutting-edge (super-resolution) microscopy and analysis tools, establish new classes of fluorescence probes and apply them directly to address relevant questions in molecular and cell biology.
